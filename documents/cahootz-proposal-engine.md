# Cahootz Proposal Engine

The Cahootz Proposal Engine is the product area for turning member ideas into reviewable, fundable, and auditable cooperative decisions.

## Purpose

- Help members submit clear proposals with goals, budget, timeline, and expected outcomes.
- Route proposals through the right review path.
- Support transparent scoring, discussion, revisions, voting, and decision history.
- Make funded work easier to monitor after approval.

## Core Flow

```text
Submit proposal
Review structure and completeness
Score against active co-op configuration
Open comment and revision window
Route to auto-review, admin review, or member vote
Record decision and funding status
Track outcomes
```

## Proposal Data

A useful proposal should capture:

- Title and summary
- Category
- Requested budget
- Timeline and milestones
- Expected member, business, or treasury impact
- Risks and dependencies
- Evidence, attachments, or links

## Review Modes

- Completeness review: checks whether the submission has enough information to evaluate.
- Policy review: compares the request against the co-op's current rules and configuration.
- Expert review: lets admins or trusted reviewers add specialized feedback.
- Member review: opens discussion and voting when a proposal needs collective approval.

## Scoring

The scoring model should remain configurable per co-op. Until a full Cahootz charter exists, scoring should rely on explicit settings stored in the co-op configuration rather than hard-coded mission language.

Recommended score groups:

- Clarity
- Budget realism
- Operational feasibility
- Member benefit
- Treasury impact
- Risk level
- Evidence quality

## Transparency

Proposal actions should leave an audit trail:

- Submission time
- Review status
- Comments and revisions
- Scores and decision rationale
- Vote totals when applicable
- Funding and milestone updates
